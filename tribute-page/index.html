Dr. A .P. J. Abdul Kalam

Here is a snapshot of Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life.

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamilnadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from University of Madras.
  • 1955: Moved to Madras to study aeronotical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology and joned DRDO.
  • 1969: Dr. Kalam was transferred to ISRO where he was project director of Indias Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV).
  • 1970 - 1980: Worked on advance missile programme. Under his leadership missiles like AGNI, PRITHVI has been delivered successfully.
  • 1981: Received Padma Bhushan award.
  • 1990: Received Padma Vibhushan award.
  • 1992 - 1999: Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and Secratory of DRDO. Pokhran-2 nuclear test aslo conducted during this period.
  • 1997: Received Bharat Ratna award.
  • 1998: He collaborated with cardiologist Soma Raju to develope low cost coronary stent, known as "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 2002: Dr. Kalam became India's 11th President. Served for only one term, which ended in 2007.
  • 2012: Dr Kalam and Dr Soma Raju designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural area, known as "Kalam-Raju-Tablet".
  • 2015: Died at the of 83.
